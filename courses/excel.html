    Introduction to Excel

    📌 What is Excel?

    The world's most popular spreadsheet software. Used for storing, organizing, and analyzing data.

    graph TD Input_Data --> Spreadsheet Spreadsheet --> Formulas Spreadsheet --> Visualization

    Cells & Ranges

    Cell: Intersection of a Row and Column (e.g., A1).

    Range: A group of cells (e.g., A1:B10).

    Formatting

    Making data readable. Bold, Italic, Cell Color, Borders.

    Conditional Formatting: Highlight top 10% or values above average.

    Formulas Intro

    All formulas start with =.

    =A1 + A2

    Basic Functions

    • =SUM(A1:A10)
    • =AVERAGE(A1:A10)
    • =MIN(A1:A10)
    • =MAX(A1:A10)

    Logic Functions

    Making decisions.

    =IF(A1>10, "Pass", "Fail")
=COUNTIF(A1:A10, ">50")

    VLOOKUP / XLOOKUP

    Finding data across tables.

    =VLOOKUP(lookup_value, table_array, col_index, [range_lookup])

    Pivot Tables

    The most powerful tool in Excel. Summarize huge datasets with drag-and-drop.

    Example: Total Sales per Region.

    Charts & Graphs

    Visualizing data.

    • Column Chart: Comparisons.
    • Line Chart: Trends over time.
    • Pie Chart: Parts of a whole.

    Sorting & Filtering

    Sort A-Z, Z-A, or by Value. Filter to hide unwanted data.

    Data Validation

    Control what users can type into a cell (e.g., Dropdown Lists).

    Macros (VBA)

    Recording repetitive tasks to automate them.
